The Question

"If UM terminates partnerships with institutions that violate human rights, why do we contract with vendors that do?"

UM's HRDD Framework already provides the methodology.

We just need to extend it to vendors.

— HRDD Framework, Section 1.2

🔗 Built-In Consistency

"The ethical expert [...] is a member of each sub-committee, ensuring that similar ethical considerations are taken into account."

— HRDD Framework, Section 1.2

Why This Matters for DSDD

  • Same ethical standards across all assessments
  • No risk of inconsistent decisions
  • Hebrew U standard = Microsoft standard

📄 Contract Clause: Ready to Adapt

HRDD Framework, Section 4.1 (Preferred option)

"Either Party is entitled to prematurely terminate this Agreement immediately [...] in the event that there have been serious indications that the party has contributed to serious human rights violations"

DSDD Extension:

Add: "...or the vendor becomes subject to extraterritorial data access orders conflicting with EU law; or the vendor is acquired by an entity failing UM's digital sovereignty assessment criteria."

💡 The Consistency Argument

"We approach global engagement with 'yes, unless we have major concerns'. UM encourages global engagement [...] as international collaboration is crucial for the quality of our research and teaching."

— HRDD Framework, Section 1.1

DSDD Translation

Same principle for vendors: "yes, unless we have major concerns."

Apply same scrutiny we give academic partners.
