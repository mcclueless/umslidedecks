Notable Finding

Static assets, not pages, dominate.

The majority of AI bot requests target JavaScript bundles and web fonts rather than HTML content pages. This suggests bots are executing full browser-like asset fetches — not just reading content — inflating bandwidth well beyond what page views alone would suggest.
Maastricht University

Googlebot Anomaly — April 2nd

140,913 requests in a single crawl period on April 2nd — a spike roughly 5–7× Googlebot's typical daily rate.

Possible causes: sitemap re-submission, major site structure change, or a scheduled deep recrawl triggered by a content update. Monitoring is recommended to distinguish routine indexing from an unintended trigger.

What Changed & Why It Matters

▲
2.5× Traffic Explosion in 30 Days
From 1.08M to 2.70M requests — AI indexing of UM web properties is accelerating rapidly. Infrastructure load, CDN costs, and rate-limiting policies should be reviewed.
▲
Bytespider (TikTok) Surges to #1
Share jumped from 15.85% → 45.9% (+627% in absolute requests). TikTok is aggressively crawling UM assets — likely for training data. Consider robots.txt policy review.
▼
OAI-SearchBot Declines Significantly
Down from 11.33% → ~2.5% (−45% absolute). May indicate OpenAI has already indexed key UM content, or a shift in crawling strategy for ChatGPT Search.
⚠
Bandwidth Nearly Doubled
87 GB → 206 GB. Bot-driven bandwidth is now a measurable cost and performance factor. Asset-level crawling (JS/fonts) amplifies per-visit data transfer.
Maastricht University

Recommended Next Steps

  1. Review & Update robots.txt
    Evaluate whether Bytespider (TikTok) and other aggressive crawlers should be rate-limited or disallowed from asset paths. Coordinate with Legal and Digital Strategy.
  2. Investigate the April 2 Googlebot Spike
    Determine the trigger — sitemap resubmission, content change, or an anomaly. Confirm it aligns with expected SEO activity.
  3. Audit Static Asset Serving
    JS bundles and fonts should not be accessible to non-browser bots at scale. Evaluate CDN-level bot filtering for static asset paths.
  4. Establish Monthly AI Traffic Baseline
    The 2.5× month-over-month growth suggests this trend will continue. Set up automated dashboards and alerts for sustained monitoring.
  5. Engage Web Governance on AI Crawling Policy
    This data supports a broader conversation about UM's policy stance on AI training data access. Proactive policy > reactive mitigation.
