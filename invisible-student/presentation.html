⭐ The RSS Trick

/rss.xml is a machine-readable list of every article we've ever published. By fetching that one file, a bot gets a complete menu of all our news content — then comes back for the full text of each item.

6,064 requests = a bot subscribed to our newsroom.

AI vs Humans: Different Interests

Humans head straight for Master's programmes and login pages.

Bots go for feeds, homepages, and news — systematic entry points to harvest the most content from the fewest requests.

🕵️ "Mystery Shoppers" for AI

Third-party tools that ask AI the questions your prospective students might ask

How It Works: The Sampling Process

1. Define prompts
Hundreds of questions your audience might ask:

"best psychology programme Netherlands"
"Maastricht vs Utrecht university"
"PBL education Europe"
"affordable masters Netherlands 2026"

2. Run prompts across AI platforms
ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity — automatically, daily

3. Aggregate into dashboards
Share of voice, trends, competitor gaps, alerts

🛒 → 🤖

"It's like a mystery shopper — but instead of visiting your store, they're asking AI about you."

What You Learn:

  • Share of Voice — "UM mentioned in 23% of relevant queries"
  • Trends — "Mentions dropped 8% last month"
  • Competitors — "TU Delft cited 2× more for STEM"
  • Misinformation — "AI says tuition is €15k — it's €2.5k!"

✓ What Can We Do?

  • Be THE source — If AI learns from us, make sure our content is authoritative, clear, and up-to-date
  • Monitor AI answers — Regularly ask ChatGPT/Gemini/Perplexity about UM and check what they say
  • Track AI bot traffic — We already have 1M+ data points monthly. ICTS report = ready to go
  • Track AI referrals — Add chatgpt.com to a custom "AI Referrers" GA4 channel group
  • Direct channels — Email, events, campus visits become MORE valuable (we own that data)
  • First-party data — Encourage sign-ups, downloads, registrations — things AI can't intercept
