🧠 How AI Answers Questions

For most users, AI feels like magic. But there's a process we can influence:

👤 Student asks
"Best psychology in NL?"
→
🤖 AI receives
the question
→
🔍 AI decides:
Search or Memory?
→
📥 AI gathers
information
→
💬 AI generates
response

📚 From Memory

AI uses its training data (months/years old)

Fast, but potentially outdated.

Our influence: Limited — already baked in

🌐 From Live Search

AI searches the web in real-time

Fresh data, cites sources. SEO still matters!

Our influence: HIGH — optimize and get cited

🎯 Key insight: The "gather information" step is our leverage point

⚙️ The Technical Flow

How modern AI actually processes a query — and where we fit in:

📅 Pre-2024 Models (Memory Only)

1
User prompt received
2
+ System prompt + Model parameters
3
= Generated answer (from training data only)

❌ No way to influence in real-time

📅 2024+ Models (Search-Augmented)

1
User query received & tokenized
2
AI decides: "Do I need fresh info?"
3
🔍 FETCH: Search → Retrieve web content
4
Inject context + Generate with citations

✓ Step 3 is where we can be found!

"The FETCH step is the door. If we're not there when AI looks, we're not in the conversation."

🎯 Zone 2: Our Sweet Spot (About Us + Search)

Questions about UM where AI searches live — highest control, best ROI:

Content Strategy

  • Facts-first writing: "#1 in NL (Keuzegids 2025)" not "world-class"
  • Stats in opening paragraphs
  • Quotable, verifiable claims
  • Create "Facts & Figures" page

Technical Optimization

  • llms.txt — curated facts for AI
  • Schema markup (Organization, FAQ)
  • Verify Bing indexing (ChatGPT uses Bing!)
  • Allow AI bots in robots.txt

Testing & Monitoring

  • Collect real questions from fairs, call center
  • Test on ChatGPT, Google AI, Perplexity
  • Score with rubric (accuracy, completeness)
  • Track competitor responses

📄 llms.txt Example

# Maastricht University > Young, international research university founded 1976 in the Netherlands. ## Quick Facts - Students: 18,500+ | 50% international - Teaching: Problem-Based Learning (PBL) — 18 students per tutorial - Ranked #1 Psychology in NL (Keuzegids 2025)

⚖️ Zone 4: The Comparison Battlefield (Include Us + Search)

Comparative queries where AI searches live — influence through sources:

"Best psychology programmes in the Netherlands 2026" · "Compare UM vs UvA"

The Challenge

AI pulls from whatever sources rank. Those might be:

🟢 Our site — Full control
🟡 Partners (StudyPortals) — Some influence
🔵 Wikipedia — Medium (editable)
🟠 Rankings/Press — Hard
🔴 Competitors — No control

Action Plan

  • Discover: Run search queries AI would make
  • Map: Which URLs appear consistently?
  • Track: What does AI actually cite?
  • Optimize: Focus on sources we can influence

Key levers:

StudyPortals profile ✓ · Wikipedia accuracy ✓ · Review campaigns

📚 Zone 1: The Memory Problem (About Us + Memory)

Factual questions where AI answers from training data — low control, but strategic options:

"When was UM founded?" · "What is PBL?" · "How old is Maastricht University?"

The Problem

  • AI "remembers" info from training (2+ years old)
  • Can't edit what's already baked in
  • Timeline: 6-18 months for changes

🌶️ SPICY INSIGHT:

If AI has LESS stale content from our site in training...

...it's MORE likely to search for fresh info!

Action Plan

  • Block training crawlers (GPTBot, Google-Extended)
  • Audit authoritative sources: Wikipedia, Wikidata
  • Fix errors with neutral, sourced edits
  • Wait & track — re-test after major model updates

🗃️ Zone 3: Third-Party Influence (Include Us + Memory)

General comparative questions from training data — influence what others say about us:

"Why is Dutch education good?" · "Best PBL universities?" · "Top international unis Europe?"

1. Map Associations

What keywords should trigger UM?

  • PBL / Problem-Based Learning
  • International university
  • European education
  • Innovation in teaching

Test now: Ask AI these topics. Are we mentioned?

2. Influence Sources

These feed AI training:

  • Wikipedia topic pages (not just our own!)
  • Rankings (THE, QS, Keuzegids)
  • Press coverage with UM framing
  • Academic papers citing UM

3. Content Saturation

Dominate the topic space:

  • Publish content connecting UM to keywords
  • Guest posts on third-party platforms
  • Thought leadership, conferences

Goal: When anyone writes about PBL, UM is the example

Key insight: We can't control what gets trained, but we can control what third-party sources say about us.

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • The "fetch" step is our leverage point — When AI searches, be there
  • Zone 2 is our sweet spot — Specific questions about UM, highest control
  • Facts beat fluff — "#1 in NL (Keuzegids 2025)" > "world-class programme"
  • Optimize for Bing too — ChatGPT uses Bing, not Google
  • Monitor or be surprised — Test what AI says monthly
